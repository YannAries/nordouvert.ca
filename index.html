Nos services

Community

As cofounders of the Canadian Open Government Civil Society Network , we are invested in fostering a large open data community by building a diverse, dynamic and sustainable ecosystem locally and on a global scale to demonstrate the economic and social benefits of open data. Our role as an active stakeholder and leader in the community allows us to advocate and promote data standards and best practices in a variety of different ways, including participating on juries, counseling tech projects, as well as coorganizing events such as the Canadian Open Data Summit and the International Open Data Day. Our goal is simple: to work together with both the public and social sectors in order to bring innovation, cuttingedge technology and transparency to their operations.

Government

Through our diverse methods of applied research, we provide local governments with open data roadmaps to get them where they want and need to go. From studies on stakeholders’ needs, like the Open Data 150, to impact assessments to key recommendation reports , we offer not only the strategic insight to innovating systems, but a list of tools as well. With Citizen Budget, OpenData511, our local open data package and APIs such as this one, we have a wealth of experience developing standards and putting them into practice in ways that have a concrete impact on everyday lives. We understand that all this can sometimes feel overwhelming it is after all a big cultural shift. That’s why we also offer open data and leadership training for public servants, particularly on datadriven management.

Parliament

As a supporter of the Declaration on Open Parliamentary Openness, it is important for us to contribute to regional and international initiatives by offering strategic and technical support for open parliament action plans. That means not only conducting extensive research on legislative openness, but offering up new and meaningful ways to make legislative data more accessible. With a strong record in building websites like OpenHouse Nova Scotia and creating groundbreaking tools like ReadyReckoner, we have quickly become Canada’s leading open data nonprofit.
